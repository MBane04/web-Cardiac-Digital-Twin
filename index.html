Project Overview

The Interactive Left Atrium Model is a GPU-accelerated N-body computational simulation that enables real-time, interactive exploration of cardiac arrhythmias. Implemented in CUDA C++ with OpenGL visualization, this project combines physics-based cardiac muscle modeling with interactive ablation capabilities for both research and medical education.

Published: Journal of Electrocardiology, Volume 86 (2024), Article 153762 | DOI: 10.1016/j.jelectrocard.2024.153762

GPU-Accelerated

CUDA implementation on NVIDIA GPUs (Dual RTX A6000) for real-time interactive simulation of thousands of cardiac myocytes

Interactive Control

Real-time ablation, pacing adjustments, and ectopic beat induction during simulation execution

Multidimensional Models

Validated progression from 1D strands to patient-specific 3D geometries from medical imaging

Clinically Relevant

Reproduces real arrhythmias (micro-reentry, flutter, SVT) and demonstrates ablation effectiveness

Quick Facts

  • Technology Stack: CUDA C++, OpenGL, Blender (Python BPY API)
  • Hardware: Dual NVIDIA RTX A6000 GPUs (48GB GDDR6 each)
  • Model Size: 11 nodes (1D) to 13,000+ nodes (anatomical LA)
  • Numerical Method: Leap-frog integration with millisecond-scale timesteps
  • Visualization: Real-time 3D rendering with node coloring, voltage mapping, recording

Clinical Background

Cardiac Arrhythmias

Irregular heartbeats affect millions worldwide and are leading contributors to stroke and heart failure. The left atrium is the primary site of complex arrhythmias, particularly atrial fibrillation (AF) and supraventricular tachycardia (SVT).

Current Treatment

Catheter ablation is the gold standard intervention, but success rates vary widely (70-80%). Procedural planning relies heavily on operator experience rather than patient-specific computational modeling.

Our Solution

An interactive digital twin enables researchers and clinicians to understand arrhythmia mechanisms, test ablation strategies, and plan procedures without patient risk—all in real time.

Cardiac Muscle Physiology

Key Distinction: Unlike skeletal muscle (1–2 ms refractory period), cardiac muscle requires 250–300 ms to recover. This long refractory period is essential: without it, sustained contraction would stop the heart. Our model accurately represents this critical physiological constraint.

Model Architecture & Validation

The model was systematically validated through dimensional progression, starting with simple geometries and advancing to anatomically accurate structures:

1

1D Strand (11 nodes)

Validated electrical wavefront propagation and conduction blockage

2

2D Ring (200–40,000 nodes)

Demonstrated macro-reentrant circuits and reentry tachycardia

3

3D Sphere (340–5,680 nodes)

Stable 3D spiral wave dynamics and multidirectional propagation

4

Idealized LA (13,000 nodes)

Anatomical features: pulmonary veins, mitral valve, Bachmann's bundle

5

Patient-Specific

Real left atrial geometries from medical imaging (CTA, MRI)

Technical Architecture

Mass-Spring System: Nodes represent cardiac myocytes; connectors transmit electrical signals with physiologically accurate refractory periods. Node mass is derived from connector properties: TM / (2 × TCL) × Σ(CL). Contractile strength follows: SR × CM (stimulus response × muscle properties).

GPU Pipeline:

  • cudaMalloc() → GPU memory allocation and setup
  • getForces() → Calculate repulsion/attraction forces
  • updateNodes() → Apply physics; advance positions
  • updateMuscles() → Propagate electrical signals; manage refractory states
  • memCopy() → Transfer results back to CPU for rendering

Research Results

The model successfully reproduces clinically-observed arrhythmias and validates therapeutic interventions:

Micro-Reentry

Small re-entrant circuits (wavelength 30–50 mm) induced through programmed pacing protocols. Successfully terminated via virtual ablation, demonstrating causal role of reentry in arrhythmia maintenance.

Atrial Flutter

Reentrant tachycardia (240–300 bpm) between pulmonary veins reproduced in anatomical models. Shows how anatomical constraints create stable reentry pathways matching clinical observations.

Ectopic Activity

Focal beats induced through continuous pacing. Demonstrates both sinus tachycardia (normal 1:1 conduction) and pathological reentrant scenarios with AV block patterns.

Virtual Ablation

Interactive ablation achieves 100% success on induced micro-reentry and variable success on anatomically-driven flutter—reflecting real clinical procedural outcomes.

Validation Metrics

Wavelength Formula: Conduction velocity (0.4 m/s) × Total cycle length (400 ms) = 160 mm minimum reentry path size.

Refractory Period: 400 ms total (200 ms contraction + 200 ms repolarization) ensures physiologically accurate arrhythmia vulnerability windows and prevents unrealistic rapid firing.

Interactive Features

Mouse-Based Interventions

  • Ablate: Block electrical conduction in selected regions
  • Ectopic Trigger: Initiate single beats from chosen locations
  • Ectopic Beat: Create recurrent pacing from specific nodes
  • Adjust Area: Modify muscle contractile properties in real time
  • Identify Node: Query individual node IDs and states

Visualization & Control

  • 3D Rotation & Zoom: Freely rotate and inspect geometry
  • Node Coloring: Voltage gradient visualization
  • Draw Front Half: Render only near half for clarity
  • Recording: Capture simulation videos for analysis
  • Beat Period Control: Adjust heart rate (millisecond precision)
  • Simulation Speed: Adjust computational rate independent of display

Data Management

  • Save/Load State: Persist and restore complete simulation configurations
  • Export Settings: Save parameters for reproducible experiments
  • Import Configuration: Load pre-defined model setups
